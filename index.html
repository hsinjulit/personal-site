Bio

Currently a master's student in Professor Shih-Yuan Chen's Advanced Antenna Laboratory (AAL) at National Taiwan University (NTU), my research focuses on polarization-agile patch antenna arrays and experimental electromagnetics in education. During the 2018–19 school year, I studied abroad at the University of Illinois at Urbana–Champaign (UIUC), and worked on coupling effects of antenna array with Professor Jennifer T. Bernhard. I received the B.S. degree in electrical engineering from NTU in 2017, where I studied circularly-polarized patch antennas at AAL and natural language understanding in Professor Lin-shan Lee's Speech Processing Laboratory.

In the summer of 2017, I worked as an RF IC Design Intern at MediaTek, automating EMI/EMC simulation workflow. Last semester, I was a graduate teaching assistant for Complex Analysis at NTU and will be one for Antenna the upcoming semester. In addition to engineering, I strive to gain interdisciplinary knowledge. At UIUC, I reconstructed Shakespeare's knowledge of 17th-century Italy and analyzed the multiple social identities of Othello with Professor Lori H. Newcomb of the Department of English as my mentor. My other interests include geosciences, sociology, theatre, and languages.

Research

Polarization-Agile Patch Antenna for Millimeter-Wave Operations

Using our proposed novel polarization-agile antenna structure, we realized the element design on psudo-packaging material at 28 GHz, and designed an antenna array with modified sequential feed at 1.575 GHz for GNSS applications.

Learn more

Near-Total Transmission of Parallel Incident Wave on PEC-Backed Water Slab

Brewster angle is the incident angle where parallel polarized incident wave fully trasmits into another dielectric. In this experiment, we used a PEC-backed water pool and realized near-total transmission of parallel wave.

Learn more

Social Differences in Shakespeare's Othello

What does 'race' mean in Shakespeare's time? Is Othello merely different from fellow Venetians in terms of skin colour, or are there more characteristics? We surveyed documents from Shakespeare's time to reconstruct his idea of Italy.
